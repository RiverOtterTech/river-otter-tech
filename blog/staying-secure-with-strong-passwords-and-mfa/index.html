Staying Secure with Strong Passwords and MFA

Small businesses are prime targets for cyber attacks, often due to outdated or weak security measures. Two key practices can significantly enhance your company’s online defenses:

Strong Passwords

  • Create complex passwords: Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid common mistakes: Don’t use obvious information like birthdays or pet names in your password.
  • Use unique passwords for each account: This minimizes the damage if one is compromised.

Multifactor Authentication (MFA)

  • What it does: MFA adds an extra layer of security by requiring more than just a password to access your accounts.
  • Types available:
    • Text message codes
    • Email verification links
    • Security apps that generate one-time codes (like Google Authenticator)
    • Fingerprint or face recognition on mobile devices
  • Why it matters: Even if a hacker gets your password, they still need this additional factor to gain access.

To set up MFA, follow these steps for common platforms:

  • Email: Enable two-factor authentication in the account settings. Follow the prompts to link a mobile number or download an authenticator app.
  • Social media and other web accounts: Check the “Security” section of your profile. Follow the on-screen instructions to enable MFA using text messages, email verification, or security apps.
  • Microsoft 365: Go to https://aka.ms/securitysetup. Follow the prompts to link a phone number or use an authenticator app for extra security.

Regularly updating your passwords and enabling MFA can protect your small business from unauthorized access. Remember, the best defense is often a combination of multiple security measures.
