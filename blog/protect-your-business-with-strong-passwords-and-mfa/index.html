Protect Your Business with Strong Passwords and MFA

Keeping your small business safe from cyber threats starts with good password hygiene and implementing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). Strong passwords and MFA can significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access to sensitive information.

Create Strong Passwords

A strong password is like a fortress protecting your digital assets. Here’s how you can create one:

  • Use Length, Not Just Complexity: Longer passwords are harder to crack than shorter ones. Aim for at least 12-15 characters.
  • Mix It Up: Combine letters (both uppercase and lowercase), numbers, and special characters to create a unique mix.
  • Avoid Common Words and Phrases: Steer clear of dictionary words or common phrases as they can be easily guessed by attackers.

Set Up Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds an extra layer of security to your accounts. Instead of relying solely on passwords, MFA requires you to provide two or more verification factors:

  • Something You Know: A password or PIN.
  • Something You Have: Your phone or a physical token that generates one-time codes.
  • Something You Are: Biometric data like your fingerprint or facial recognition (available on some devices).

To set up MFA, follow these steps for common services:

  • Email: Check the settings in Gmail, Outlook, or other email providers. Typically, you’ll receive a code via text message or an authenticator app.
  • Social Media: Platforms like Facebook and Twitter offer MFA options that require a code sent to your phone.
  • Cloud Services: Providers such as Google Drive, Dropbox, and Microsoft 365 have built-in MFA settings that can be accessed from their security settings pages.
